Bridging Two Worlds


Turing's mission is to produce simultaneously cost-efficient, easy to implement, and environmentally-friendly technologies.
Thus we made Root to make it effortless for users to achieve immediate financial savings while contributing to environmental sustainability.

The Power of Networks


One Root can already save substantial energy with minimal effort. However, energy savings grow exponentially as the number of Roots in the same network increases.
Getting a second root means the first Root is no longer limited to it's own data input, giving it more information to optimize over.
As Turing's algorithms gather more information to learn from, they'll progressively become smarter and more capable of producing cutting edge optimization metrics for your energy.

Progress

December 2015: We got the idea for Root and did all of the research to ensure that it is buildable and cost efficient.

January 2016: We won a 75+ team competition for admission to the WeissLabs Accelerator at Penn Engineering. We now have space and mentorship by industry experts.

February 2016: We built a minimum viable prototype; we are able to control the flow of electricity through an outlet via the press of a button on a computer. We looking for a summer accelerator program so that we can work full time on Root.
